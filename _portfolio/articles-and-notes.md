## Overview

This section gathers my mathematical articles, notes, and problem-solving essays.

The purpose of these articles is to build a public record of my mathematical development. I use them to practice rigorous exposition, explore difficult problems, and develop the kind of clarity required for serious independent research.

## Mathematical Workshops

The Mathematical Workshops are a series of problem-solving articles written in a detailed and pedagogical style. Each article focuses on one or more mathematical problems and explains the solution step by step.

The goal is not only to present the final answer, but also to show the reasoning behind the solution.

## Research Notes

My research notes are longer and more exploratory. They are used to investigate mathematical ideas, possible research directions, and connections between different areas of mathematics.

These notes may include incomplete ideas, technical explorations, conjectures, failed attempts, and refined arguments.

## Topics

The articles and notes may cover topics such as:

- Combinatorics
- Number theory
- Probability
- Financial mathematics
- Actuarial science
- Stochastic processes
- Generating functions
- Mathematical games
- Research problems

## Purpose

This writing project is part of a larger objective: building a strong mathematical portfolio for future research opportunities.

By writing regularly, I want to improve my ability to explain ideas clearly, structure arguments, and transform isolated problem-solving sessions into durable mathematical work.
