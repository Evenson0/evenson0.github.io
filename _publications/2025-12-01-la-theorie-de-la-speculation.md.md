## Description

This article, published in **French on Substack**, traces the historical origins of mathematical finance through Louis Bachelier’s doctoral thesis *Théorie de la spéculation* (1900). It discusses the intellectual context in which this work was written and explains why Bachelier’s thesis is now widely regarded as one of the founding texts of quantitative finance.

The article also revisits Bachelier’s life, the initial reception of his ideas, and the gradual influence of his work on the modern theory of financial markets.
